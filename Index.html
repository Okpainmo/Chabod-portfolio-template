Project Name: Releef
Releef got started at a Google DSC (developer student club) event that I attended at Bida polytechnic, Niger state, Nigeria. The event was a three day event (with two workshop days and a one day hackathon). The hackathon was to build a solution that would compete in the Google solution Challenge 2021. The solution Challenge this year (2021), was for teams to provide solutions to some global issues like gender equality, poverty and etcetera.
At the event, Everyone was asked to pitch ideas in any of the listed problem areas that we could build a solution on. That was when I got the idea to build Releef. The Releef spark came up when i was in the bathroom (i think i was bathing then). - funny right?
I then Shared the Releef idea at the Event. and we started out - building it.
Releef was to compete in the "zero poverty category".
Releef is simply a platform where people can share real and verifiable information about regions in the world that are suffering from hardships like famines, wars, the main enemy - poverty, or any other circumstance that calls for help. On Releef, people would post written details about such locations, along with clear Images. Releef was also meant to provide a Google map interface for users to supply map details of such locations. Another major feature of Releef was to be "Donation" - A payment gateway was to be provided for users to make donations to any locations of their choice.
Releef would then partner with trusted bodies like UNICEF, UNHRC and others. For proper distribution of such donations among other benefits.

Project Name: Chabod Portfolio Template
"I wanted a portfolio website to help me tell the world what I do." - That was how Chabod started out.
Chabod is a single page portfolio website that is suitable for anyone in the digital world. Whether for software/web developers, or designers or SEO geeks, Chabod will be a perfect choice.
I built Chabod for myself. But while building it, I got better plans for it. and for now, I intend scaling up Chabod for public release. The public release might be free, or for some "little gains"
Chabod is simply awesome. Loaded with simplicity, It comes with a simple black-white-grey color combination. But I intend to scale Chabod with some extra work to enable the posibility of some cool customizations, Thanks to Sass mixins... and some other tools that might come in handy to power more color options.
Chabod isn't realy a "single page" portfolio website. Its realy a four page site that saves visitors the extra page load stress by displaying the work profiles of the site owner in pop-ups powered by Javascript.
Using pop-ups also helps to keep users glued onto the site while exploring the works (project profiles) with only some simple clicks that opens and closes the pop-ups.
